The pipeline

Five agents. One document. Nothing hidden.

Most resume tools are a form with a spinner. ARS-MME shows you the machinery — each agent reports what it produced before the next one starts.

01
Ingestion
Reads your PDF, image, or pasted text — OCR included. No template to fill in; bring the resume you already have.
→ resume.pdf · 214 words
02
Verifier
Checks claimed certifications for internal consistency. Unverifiable claims block synthesis until you correct them — or sign an explicit disclaimer that travels with the document.
→ verified · confidence 0.94
03
Market Scout
Pulls live listings from public job feeds, or maps demand trends for your target industry. Live data is labeled live; sample data is labeled sample.
→ 31 live jobs · Remotive
04
Analyst
Embeds your profile and the target role — on your device when your hardware allows — then computes the ATS match score and the exact keywords you're missing.
→ ATS match 78% · 3 gaps

Writes the document against a strict schema. It never invents employers, dates, or degrees — if a section can't be supported by your input, it stays honest or stays out.

Questions

Asked and answered.

Is it free?
Yes. No account, no card, no trial clock. The project is open source — you can also self-host the whole stack with your own model keys; the deployment guide covers it end to end.
Where does my data actually go?
Your text is sent to the model provider (Gemini or NVIDIA) to generate the document, and nowhere else. Nothing is written to a database — the app doesn't have one. Session memory purges itself on close or after 15 minutes idle.
Will it invent experience I don't have?
No. The Synthesizer writes against a strict schema that forbids inventing employers, dates, degrees, or certifications. If the Verifier can't stand behind a claim, generation blocks until you correct it — or you attach a disclaimer that is typeset into the document.
What is an ATS match, and why should I care?
Applicant tracking systems parse your resume before a human reads it. The Analyst embeds your profile and the target role, computes their similarity, and lists the exact keywords you match and miss — so you fix the gaps before you send, not after the rejection.
Why can I watch the agents work?
Because trust needs evidence. Every stage reports what it produced — what was extracted, what was verified, what matched. A resume built in the open is easier to stand behind than one that appears from a spinner.
